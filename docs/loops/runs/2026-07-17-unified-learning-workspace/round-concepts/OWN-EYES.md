## Final visual findings

### A — Поля мысли

- Strongest editorial hierarchy: the mathematical statement dominates, and the semantic bracket
  ties `из оставшихся` to the localized AI comment without creating another card destination.
- Mobile feedback reads as an annotation on the same sheet; orange remains action/annotation,
  not a full-page brand wash.
- Trade-off: the linear mobile model is the least spatially novel of the three, although it is the
  calmest and easiest to scan.

### B — Точный верстак

- Strongest explicit zoning and state orientation on desktop; the work surface and evidence rail
  communicate exactly where the child is working versus asking for help.
- The preserved mobile rail makes the direction structurally distinct and keeps topic/mode state
  stable through feedback.
- Trade-off: the rail consumes 48 px on a narrow screen and makes long tutor copy feel denser than
  in A or C.

### C — Нить решения

- Strongest continuity metaphor: task, AI evidence and next action are one trace; `uncertain`
  reads as an interrupted evidence segment rather than a red mathematical error.
- Mobile proof-thread keeps feedback spatially attached without a separate destination, and the
  mint contextual surface stays subordinate to the problem.
- Trade-off: desktop distributes task, context and action across the widest canvas, which may
  increase eye travel compared with A.

## Root verdict before blind judges

No visual P0/P1 or critical rubric cap remains. A and C are the strongest candidates for the
production direction; B remains a valid structurally distinct benchmark candidate. Selection is
deferred to the two frozen-rubric blind judges.
